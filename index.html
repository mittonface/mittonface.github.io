A LITTLE ABOUT US

The City Centre Community School started in January of 2014 and is happy to be opening once again in January of 2015! It’s a a place to learn something new, meet members of the community, and most of all have fun! We welcome all members of the Charlottetown community and PEI at large to participate in a new selection of courses, starting January 6th at Colonel Gray High School. For ten weeks, instructors with various backgrounds and experience will cover topics ranging from dance and healthy living, to web development and improv acting. The school maintains a focus of providing material which hasn’t yet been offered in community schools on the Island before as well as offering longtime favourites.

With a committee led by young adults from around Charlottetown, the City Centre Community School is building on an existing community school platform, but with a different approach and focus. Our goal is to raise awareness of the community school system in PEI, increasing its participation levels and the program’s sustainability for years to come. Each class will be held on Tuesday evenings, 7:00pm – 8:30pm. This includes a short break for community time, which gives a chance for students and instructors to share what they have learned or simply enjoy provided snacks and conversation. It’s a great opportunity to mingle and meet people from around the community!

Online pre-registration has closed. You can still register for any class listed on the website at 7pm on January 6th at Colonel Gray High School

INTRODUCTION TO LATIN



Victoria Goddard

Course Description

Ever wanted to learn Latin, major language of literature, culture, and empire in western Europe for over 2000 years? Here's your chance! We'll start with basic vocabulary and grammar, and aim to be reading excerpts from real texts by the end of the course. This will be an introductory-level class, so if you've not studied a language since high school, that's fine: we'll be going over the basic rules of grammar, together with some tips on learning languages, in the first few classes. We'll also discuss some of the history and literature of the Latin language from ancient Rome to modern times. We will be using handouts rather than a textbook, but you will be expected to do some memorization.

About Victoria

Victoria Goddard is a novelist, gardener, and jobbing humanist. After finishing a stint as a teaching fellow in the Foundation Year Programme at the University of King's College (Halifax), she spent six months walking down the length of England, six months as the sexton of a church in Halifax, and moved to Charlottetown in September of this year to write and look for land. She has a PhD in Medieval Studies from the University of Toronto, where she studied Dante and Boethius (a late Roman philosopher) and medieval Latin allegory, and also taught Latin to both beginners and doctoral candidates (and sometimes both!). She's delighted to join the Community School in 2015.

 



INTERACTIVE COMPUTING



Matt Duffy

Course Description

You have regular access to a computer; you can turn on a computer; you can find, open and install new programs on your computer; you see all these programs and files and wonder how they work and how to make your own computer programs. You think: ‘all this computer stuff is nice, but how can I use it to make the changes I want to see in my environment?’ You’re ready to take the next steps but aren’t sure exactly where to begin.

If you decide to join, for a bit of context we’ll start with a whirlwind tour of the major developments in Computation and Information Systems. We’ll also talk about recent developments in the Maker movement: people who are leveraging the increasing power and decreasing cost of technology to do truly amazing things for themselves like process automation, data collection, and 3D printing.

Then you’ll gain the tools you need to do these sorts of things for yourself.

About Matt

Matt Duffy has been successfully taking things apart for decades, and has on several occasions has managed to successfully put them back together. His megalomaniacal tendencies have led to to use computers to exert control over the world around him. His gentler tendencies have led him to offer to teach these skills to his community.

 



COMMUNICATION & IMPROMPTU SPEAKING



Darlene Dunn


Sean Booth


Sarah Brown

Course Description

The class will be led by experienced Toastmasters in a fun, comfortable and supportive environment. We will cover a number of topics that will help students learn to communicate effectively, including the following:

  • Take the Terror Out of a Talk
  • Organize your message
  • Construct a Speech
  • How to Relate to Your Audience
  • Gestures
  • Vocal Variety
  • Using Visual Aids

Some of the benefits to the participants attending the class would be:

  • Learn to communicate clearly
  • Learn to communicate with confidence
  • Ace job interviews
  • Organize their messages and learn how to present their ideas
  • Learn to think & communicate "quick on your feet" 

About the Toastmasters

Darlene Dunn has been a Toastmaster for almost 7 years. She is currently the club President of Premiere Toastmasters and also belongs to Spotlight Toastmasters which is an advanced Toastmasters club. Darlene is excited to share what she has learned from the Toastmasters program and credits Toastmasters for opening many doors both professionally and personally!

Sean Booth is a Product Line Manager for Carestream Health where he manages a business unit in the healthcare industry.  He currently lives in Greenwich PEI, and has been an active member of Charlottetown Premiere Toastmasters for the past three years.   Sean serves as the VP Education at the Toastmasters club, and is a board member of the PEI French Language School Board.  Sean enjoys speaking and the art of communicating, and as has had the fortune to give both sales and educational presentations all over the world.

Sarah Brown has been a member of Toastmasters International for more than 10 years and has held various executive positions within her home club, Premiere Toastmasters. In addition to public speaking, Sarah has dabbled in improv, and even stand-up comedy. 

 



TRANSITIONING TOWARDS A PLANT-BASED LIFESTYLE



Hilary Wood

Course Description

Eating real, whole, plant-based foods is essential in life. This course will not only teach you about transitioning into a vegetarian or vegan lifestyle, but will also discuss the modern food industry and its problems.

Topics that will be covered in this course:

  • reasons to go plant-based
  • myths of vegetarian/vegan diets
  • nutrition tips
  • healthy meal planning
  • getting back to the basics of real food
  • more!

About Hilary

Having recently graduated from the Holistic Nutrition program at the Canadian School of Natural Nutrition in Moncton, New Brunswick, Hilary Wood brings her knowledge of nutrition to the City Centre Community School this winter. Along with her education, Hilary has personal experience with living a plant-based lifestyle, as she was vegetarian for most of her life and is now a vegan who is very passionate on the subject of living a healthy, animal-product free life.

 



THE FUN OF IMPROV DRAMA



Ruth Lacey

Course Description

The main goal of the classes will be to have fun and gradually AND GENTLY build up the skills of creating a team, and learning to think on one's feet and creatively and imaginatively problem solve. The games cause participants to lose themselves in laughter, confidence and joy through the fun of sorting out the problems in pairs or groups. Examples of some of the beginning exercises/ games would be: mirror exercise, slide show, sound scape, conducted story, and what am I doing?

About Ruth

Ruth Lacey has been teaching Improv to all ages for 12 years. Her groups have performed and competed publicly with great success. She was in both Murder in The Cathedral and Jesus Christ Superstar with ACT in 2004 after which she decided to devote her time to teaching and helping others create their own material. Her Improv teachers were Laurie Murphy, founder and leader of F.I.G.H.T. Improv Troupe, and Rob Thompson of Sketch-22 and other Improv comedy troupes.

EXPLORING YOGA & MEDITATION



Kirstin Sweet

Pre-registration for this course has finished!
But don't worry! We saved some slots for in-person registration on opening night. If you show up to Colonel Gray on January 6th, you might still be able to register!

Course Description

Throughout the ten weeks we will explore many different styles of yoga and meditation. Focusing on the connection of the body and breath, each week we will move through a series of asanas (poses) tailored to the interests and abilities of students. We will pay special attention to correct alignment in the asanas and control of the breath. Classes will cater to all levels of experience. As yoga involves much more than physical movement, the second half of class will focus on different meditation techniques and their connection to our daily lives. Meditation is the practice of calming and focusing the mind. Through forms of active and passive meditation we will work on developing mindfulness.

About Kirstin

Kirstin Sweet has been practicing various styles of yoga for several years. She has developed a true passion for the practice and believes that absolutely everyone can do yoga. In 2014, Kirstin traveled to India and completed a yoga teacher training course in the foothills of the Himalayas. There she learned how to effectively lead students in many styles of yoga and discovered many different meditation techniques. She is excited to share her knowledge and passion for yoga with the students of City Centre Community School!

WRITING THE SHORT STORY



Cindy Lapeña

Course Description

Not for the faint-hearted, Writing the Short Story is a workshop course for participants who want to learn how to write a short story by experiencing the actual process of writing one (or several!). They will learn a little about the history of short stories, and everything they need to know about what is needed to write a good short story. After the first meeting, participants will experience several writing exercises that will result in the writing of a short short story (often called flash fiction) or a component of a story. Works will be published online in The Writing Pool (unless otherwise declared).

About Cindy

Cindy Lapeña returns to the City Centre Community School to teach a course on Writing the Short Story. A published writer, blogger, and owner/publisher of The Writing Pool, Cindy has extensive teaching experience at the high school and university levels. As a training consultant, she has delivered numerous seminars and workshop courses on a variety of topics. She holds a Master of Arts degree in English Literature with a focus on Creative Writing and Drama. In 2014, she taught How to Write a Novel in 10 Days at the City Centre Community School.

 



CULTIVATING RESILIENCE IN YOUR COMMUNITY


Pre-registration for this course has finished!
But don't worry! We saved some slots for in-person registration on opening night. If you show up to Colonel Gray on January 6th, you might still be able to register!

Course Description

This course will introduce you to ideas and learning resources for community resilience. Guest speakers and instructors will facilitate a collaborative enquiry with students into how we might cope with the challenges we face today and make the transition to a world that works. Topics we will explore together include sustainable energy solutions, creating food systems that work, identifying transportation possibilities, future proofing our homes and whole systems thinking. You can look forward to learning, thinking, conversing and creating in every class with a group of like-minded individuals.

About The Facilitators

This course will be facilitated by a dynamic, passionate group of action-oriented individuals including farmers, foodies, researchers, educators, entrepreneurs and other visionaries. Together, they share a vision of PEI as a prosperous and sustainable Island community, boldly navigating a challenging future. Each of them looks forward to sharing their unique knowledge, skills and energy, while also connecting with and learning from course participants.

Course facilitators include: Mathieu Arsenault, Shannon Courtney, Phil Ferraro, Adam MacLean, Jordan MacPhee, Lyndsay Moffatt, Carla Morgan, Lisa Steele, and Allison Walsh.
